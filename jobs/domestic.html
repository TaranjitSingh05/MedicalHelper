Domestic Workers

Health risks and preventive measures for domestic helpers and caregivers.

About this job

Domestic work involves cleaning, cooking, washing, childcare and eldercare. Hazards include exposure to strong cleaning agents, slips and falls on wet floors, lifting-related strain, and social isolation. Safe practices reduce risks.

Common Diseases and Health Risks

Medium

Chemical Irritation

Skin and respiratory irritation due to cleaning agents and aerosols.

Medium

Slips, Trips & Falls

Wet surfaces and cluttered areas increase fall risk while working.

Low

Musculoskeletal Strain

Back and joint pain from repetitive scrubbing, lifting and bending.

Low

Allergies & Asthma

Dust, perfumes, or pet dander can trigger respiratory issues.
