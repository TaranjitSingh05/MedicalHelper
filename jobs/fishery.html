Fishery Workers

Health risks and prevention strategies for workers involved in fishing, processing, and marine operations.

About this job

Fishery work includes offshore and onshore activities such as catching, sorting, and processing fish. Workers face risks from rough seas, sharp tools, slippery decks, cold exposure, and long working hours. Preparedness and safety protocols are critical.

Common Diseases and Health Risks

High

Drowning & Overboard Falls

Accidents at sea due to rough weather, slippery decks, or lack of life vests.

Medium

Cuts & Punctures

Injuries from hooks, knives, and processing equipment leading to infections.

Medium

Water-borne Infections

Exposure to contaminated water causing bacterial or parasitic infections.

Low

Hypothermia & Cold Stress

Cold exposure during early mornings and at sea without proper clothing.

Low

Sunburn & Dehydration

Long hours under the sun leading to skin damage and heat-related issues.
