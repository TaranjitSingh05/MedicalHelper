Construction Workers

Comprehensive health risks and prevention strategies for construction workers.

About this job

Construction work involves physical tasks such as building, repairing, and maintaining structures. Workers are often exposed to heights, heavy machinery, dust, chemicals, heat, and noise. Proper safety measures and awareness can significantly reduce health risks.

Common Diseases and Health Risks

High

Falls from Height

Serious injuries due to falls from scaffolding, ladders, or roofs.

High

Equipment Injuries

Lacerations and crush injuries from heavy machinery and tools.

Medium

Respiratory Issues (Dust)

Long-term lung problems from cement, silica, and asbestos dust.

Medium

Heat Stress

Dehydration, heat exhaustion, and heat stroke during summer months.

Low

Noise-Induced Hearing Loss

Hearing damage due to prolonged exposure to loud equipment.
